IMPORTANCE OF CAREER COUNSELLING

Career Counselling is a process that focuses on helping one understand one’s own self, as well as work trends so that one can take an informed decision about career and education. Your career decision is a process that, whether you know it or not, actually started when you were born! There are a number of factors that influence your career development, including your interests, abilities, ethics and values, personality, family background, and surroundings. Career Counselling is a process that will help you to know and understand yourself properly and the world of work in order to make best-fit career choices and take an educational and career as well as life decisions. Career development is truly a lifelong process, meaning that throughout your life will change, situations will change, and you will continually have to make a proper career choice. The goal of Career Counselling is to not only help the applicants make the decisions they need to make now but to give them the knowledge and skills, they need to make their future career and life decisions.

WHY CAREER COUNSELLING?

Every student is unique, different and expresses specific strengths, weaknesses and interest. It is really important to pay undivided attention to every student's career plans from a very young age. They have multiple preferences, skills, abilities and goals in life and when it’s about career choices same with multiple subjects, streams and colleges, it is too stressful for a student to analyse the pros and cons and manage all these things at the same time. That's why they required a professional Career Counsellor to assist in selecting the right career choices! The process of analysing a student's psychological state of mind, interests and personality with various counselling methods like Career Assessment Test or Psychometric Test & this technique helps the student have some clarity about his/her future.

WHAT IS THE CAREER ASSESSMENT TEST?

Career Assessment Test is a technique that is designed to help the students to understand how a variety of personal attributes (i.e. interests, preferences, abilities, aptitudes, personality, communication skills and more), impact their potential success and satisfaction with different career opportunities and find the best fit career for them. The career assessment test concludes with a comprehensive career assessment report that discusses the most important conclusions. The report gives a clear overview of the student abilities, interests and talents and constitutes a sound career advice for the candidate.
